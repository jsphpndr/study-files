The Anthology of Art - Art in the Bahamas

Nowe H. Harris-Smith

February 25, 2021

Introduction - The Beginning

The first artisans were the Lucayans. They crafted primative sculptural works such as duhos and zemis. These artworks, were the first discovered crafts within the Bahamas. This article aims to explore the cultural diversity within the artistic diaspora.

The Evolution

During the 1800s, artists such as Winslow Homer began visiting Nassau to paint. Through his unique eye, he captured the Bahamian environment through watercolors. The technique of landscape or seascape painting continued throughout generations and was perfected by artists such as Brent Malone, Chan Pratt, and Eddie Minnis. By the 1960s, many Bahamian artisans were exploring ceramic techniques at the historic Chelsea Pottery. These diverse practices pioneered some of the artwork explored today.

Conclusion - The New Wave

Today many artists embrace multimedia practices that combine traditional fine art skills with new media. For young artisans, Bahamian art does not only encompass seascapes but also ideolgies and abstract ideas. My current practice implements illustrave techniques along with graphic design principles to explore the concept of identity. Art is diverse, and continues to evolve within Bahamian society.
